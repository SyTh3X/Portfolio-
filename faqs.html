Our Story

A small workshop's idea of what a sofa should be

Winslow & Rye started with a fairly ordinary complaint: too many sofas are either beautifully photographed and poorly made, or well made and dressed up in a showroom experience nobody has time for. We wanted neither — just honestly built furniture, shown plainly, ordered by talking to an actual person.

We're named for two English towns rather than a founder, because the sofas were always meant to be the point, not a personality. Each piece is built to order by upholsterers who've spent years learning where a frame needs an extra joint and where a seam needs a second row of stitching — decisions that don't show up in a product photo but do show up eight years later.

Built to be kept

Kiln-dried timber, corner-blocked frames, and cushion fills chosen for how they perform in year five, not just on delivery day.

Made by hand

Every sofa passes through the hands of an upholsterer, not just a production line — which is also why small batches sometimes vary very slightly.

Talked about, not sold

No checkout funnel, no abandoned-cart emails — just a WhatsApp conversation with someone who can actually answer a question about fabric.
